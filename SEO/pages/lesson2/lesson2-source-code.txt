<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What does afwan mean in Lebanese Arabic?</h2>



<p><strong>Afwan</strong>&nbsp;most commonly means&nbsp;<strong>“excuse me”</strong>&nbsp;or&nbsp;<strong>“pardon”</strong>&nbsp;in everyday Lebanese Arabic. In some Arabic contexts, it can also be used like&nbsp;<strong>“you’re welcome”</strong>, but in this lesson we focus on the way Lebanese speakers use&nbsp;<em>afwan</em>&nbsp;in real travel and everyday situations.</p>



<p><strong>Quick meaning:</strong>&nbsp;<em>afwan</em>&nbsp;= excuse me / pardon</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Transcript</h2>



<p>In this lesson, you&#8217;ll learn some useful phrases for traveling in Lebanon.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">At the Airport</h3>



<p>Upon arrival in Lebanon, you will hear the greeting &#8220;Welcome to Lebanon&#8221; which in Arabic is &#8220;Ahla w sahla b Lebnen.&#8221;</p>



<p>If your baggage is late, you can say &#8220;Excuse me, my baggage is late&#8221; in Arabic, which is &#8220;afwan, ghradeh t2akharit.&#8221; Alternatively, you can also say &#8220;afwan shenateh t2akharit.&#8221;</p>



<p>If you&#8217;ve lost your baggage, you can say &#8220;Excuse me, I lost my baggage&#8221; in Arabic, which is &#8220;afwan dayya3et ghradeh&#8221; or &#8220;afwan dayya3et shenateh.&#8221;</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Exchanging Money</h3>



<p>If you need to <a href="https://lebanese-arabic.com/lesson4/" type="link" id="https://lebanese-arabic.com/lesson4/">exchange money</a>, you can say &#8220;Excuse me, I want to exchange money&#8221; in Arabic, which is &#8220;afwan, baddeh sarrif masareh.&#8221; The currency in Lebanon is the Lira, and both Lira and dollars are accepted in shops. Euros are not accepted.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Taking a Cab</h3>



<p>To take a cab to your hotel, step outside the airport and you&#8217;ll see many cabs available. There are no buses or metros available in the airport.</p>



<p>When you get into the cab, you can say &#8220;Hello, I want to go to <a href="https://lebanese-arabic.com/sassine/">Ashrafieh</a>&#8221; in Arabic, which is &#8220;<a href="https://lebanese-arabic.com/lesson1/" type="link" id="https://lebanese-arabic.com/lesson1/">marhaba</a> baddeh rouh aal Ashrafieh.&#8221; To ask how much the cab will cost, you can say &#8220;How much do you take?&#8221; in Arabic, which is &#8220;Addeh betrid.&#8221;</p>



<p>Note that the first cab you take from the airport will likely be more expensive than subsequent cabs. There are two types of cabs in Lebanon: &#8220;service&#8221; cabs which you see on the streets, and &#8220;taxi&#8221; cabs which you call to reserve in advance. Service cabs are much cheaper than taxi cabs and will cost less than one euro or dollar.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">When do you use afwan in Lebanese Arabic?</h2>



<p>You can use&nbsp;<strong>afwan</strong>&nbsp;when you want to politely get someone’s attention, say&nbsp;<strong>“excuse me”, or ask for help in a public place. It is especially useful while traveling in Lebanon, for example at the airport, while exchanging money, or when speaking to a taxi driver.</strong></p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Common uses of afwan</h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Excuse me</strong>: to politely start a question</li>



<li><strong>Pardon</strong>: when you want someone to repeat something</li>



<li><strong>You’re welcome</strong>: possible in broader Arabic usage, but less central to this lesson than the “excuse me” meaning</li>
</ul>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Afwan vs. “thank you” in Lebanese Arabic</h2>



<p><strong>Afwan</strong>&nbsp;does&nbsp;<strong>not</strong>&nbsp;primarily mean “thank you.” If you want to learn how to say&nbsp;<strong>thank you in Lebanese Arabic</strong>, see&nbsp;<a href="https://lebanese-arabic.com/lesson40/">this lesson on thank you in Lebanese</a>. This page focuses on the meaning and use of&nbsp;<strong>afwan</strong>&nbsp;as&nbsp;<strong>excuse me or&nbsp;pardon.</strong></p>
