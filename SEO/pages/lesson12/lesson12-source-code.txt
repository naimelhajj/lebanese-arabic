<h2 class="wp-block-heading">How do you say grandmother and grandfather in Lebanese Arabic?</h2>



<p>In Lebanese Arabic, family words can vary depending on the family and region, but this lesson helps you understand the most common words used for&nbsp;<strong>grandmother</strong>,&nbsp;<strong>grandfather</strong>, and other relatives.</p>



<p><strong>Quick answer:</strong>&nbsp;this page explains common Lebanese Arabic words for&nbsp;<strong>grandmother</strong>,&nbsp;<strong>grandfather</strong>, and close family members.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">About Uncles and Aunts</h3>



<p>Now, uncle. If by “uncle” I want to say the brother of my father I say 3ammeh. If I want to say uncle as the brother of my mother, I say khaleh.</p>



<p>The same for aunt. It’s 3amteh from my father’s side, and from my mother’s side it’s khalteh.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Cousins in Lebanese Arabic</h3>



<p><strong>My cousin</strong>: I tell you good news, you can also say cousin, because it gets tricky.</p>



<p>About the cousin: I want to say I’m going to my cousin’s, then I would say ana ray7a 3end cousinteh.</p>
