### Analysis of `/home-v2/`

The variant page `/home-v2/` is a step in the right direction but remains strategically flawed.

*   **What it does right:** It introduces a direct Call-To-Action (CTA) high on the page: "Ready to Start Speaking Lebanese?" with a "Book Your $15 Trial Lesson" button. This is a significant improvement over the control homepage. It also surfaces testimonials earlier.
*   **Where it fails:** It's a hybrid page that doesn't commit to a primary goal. It introduces a transactional element but immediately dilutes it with multiple other exploration paths ("Youtube Videos," "Books," "Online Classes") and the very long "Our Story" section. For a user who searched "lebanese arabic course," you are asking them to consider buying, then immediately distracting them with four other things to do and a 500-word essay.

The user journey is still confused. It's like a store entrance that has a prominent "Buy Now" sign, but also four other doors leading to a library, a cinema, and a museum.

### Strategic Recommendation for `/home-v2/`

To align with high-intent organic traffic, we must restructure `/home-v2/` to function as a **"Conversion-First, Exploration-Second"** landing page. The goal is to satisfy the user's primary intent (to learn/buy) immediately, while still allowing for exploration in a deliberate, secondary manner.

Here is the blueprint. Re-order and modify `/home-v2/` to follow this exact structure:

**1. The "5-Second Test" Module (Above the Fold)**
This is the most critical section. It must immediately confirm the user is in the right place and give them a clear path forward.

*   **Eliminate:** The generic `<h2>Lebanese Arabic with Hiba</h2>` and "Experience..." tagline. This is redundant and wastes valuable headline space.
*   **New Headline (H1):** `<h1>Learn to Speak Lebanese Arabic with Live, 1-on-1 Lessons</h1>`
    *   **Rationale:** This directly matches the user's search intent and clearly states the product.
*   **New Sub-headline:** `<h2>Book a $15 risk-free trial lesson with a native teacher and start speaking from day one.</h2>`
    *   **Rationale:** It presents the offer, the key benefit (speaking from day one), and reduces risk ("risk-free").
*   **Primary CTA Button:** The button text must be compelling and clear.
    *   **Change From:** "Book Your $15 Trial Lesson"
    *   **Change To:** `Book Your Risk-Free Trial`
    *   **Rationale:** "Risk-Free" is a more powerful psychological trigger than repeating the price, which is already in the sub-headline. The destination must be `/lebanese-lessons/`.
*   **Visual:** Keep the hero image, it's effective.

**2. The "Trust-Builder" Module (Immediate Social Proof)**
Directly below the fold, you must build immediate trust.

*   **Move Up:** The "What Our Students Achieve" section.
*   **Enhance:** Instead of just text quotes, use the "Wall of Faces" concept from the `v3-final-social-proof.html` file we previously discussed. Feature 2-3 of your most powerful video testimonials here, with the student's face and a compelling quote.
*   **CTA:** Add a secondary button here: `See All Testimonials`, linking to the main testimonials page.

**3. The "How It Works" Module (Clarify the Product)**
After building trust, concisely explain the offer. This primes them for the `/lebanese-lessons/` page.

*   **Create a new, simple 3-step section:**
    1.  **Book Your Trial:** "Pick a time that works for you and book your 55-minute trial lesson for just $15."
    2.  **Meet Your Teacher:** "Get matched with one of our expert, native-speaking teachers."
    3.  **Start Speaking:** "Experience our fun, effective method and decide if it's right for you. No commitment."
*   **Rationale:** This demystifies the process and makes it feel easy and low-risk.

**4. The "Exploration" Module (The Secondary Path)**
This is where you satisfy the user's secondary need to explore, but only *after* you've presented the primary solution.

*   **Re-frame:** Change the heading from "Four Ways of Learning Lebanese" to `<h2>Explore Our Free Resources & Other Materials</h2>`.
*   **Content:** This is where the links to YouTube, Books, and the Blog belong. You are now framing them as supplementary materials, not competing choices.
*   **Remove:** The "Online Classes" link from this section. It's redundant and confusing. The single goal of this page is to drive users to the 1-on-1 trial lessons on `/lebanese-lessons/`.

**5. The "Founder's Note" Module (Final Trust Signal)**
The "Our Story" section is valuable but is far too long and placed too high.

*   **Move Down:** Place this section *after* the Exploration module.
*   **Condense:** Edit the story down to a 150-word "Founder's Note." Keep the personal touch but be ruthless in cutting text. Start with the "So, dear potential student..." paragraph and work backward. The core message is Hiba's passion, not the entire history of the business.
*   **Rationale:** It now serves as a final, personal trust signal before the final CTA, rather than a roadblock at the beginning of the journey.

**6. Final CTA Module**
Repeat the primary call to action.

*   **Add a full-width section with the headline:** `<h2>Ready to Start Your Lebanese Journey?</h2>`
*   **Add the button:** `Book Your Risk-Free Trial`
