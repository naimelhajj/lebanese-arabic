<h2 class="wp-block-heading has-text-align-center">Our Story</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Back in 2010, I had a beer at Dany's bar in <a href="https://lebanese-arabic.com/hamra/">Hamra </a>and met a group of enthusiastic Italian colleagues who had moved to Beirut for work. As we drank and laughed, they shared their disappointment with me: after studying classical-formal Arabic for years, they discovered they couldn't really use it in everyday conversation.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Lebanese people mostly use the local dialect, not formal Arabic. This is when the idea of teaching Lebanese Arabic struck me. I made the first YouTube video with the help of my friend Naim, who was able to handle video editing and more. We started recording videos and shared them with friends, initially as a joke. But that joke turned into almost a full-time occupation, thanks to the Italian group of friends! After teaching on YouTube for years with over 18,000 subscribers, the channel grew. Naim and I decided to expand it to offer online lessons.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":4232,"sizeSlug":"medium","linkDestination":"media","align":"right","className":"is-resized"} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image alignright size-medium is-resized"><a href="https://lebanese-arabic.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/79324337_3265654416838400_3944349221339004928_n.jpg"><img src="https://lebanese-arabic.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/79324337_3265654416838400_3944349221339004928_n-300x225.jpg" alt="A group class from Lebanese Arabic with Hiba, showing Hiba with three students sitting around a table, with a laptop and notebooks and coffee and cake" class="wp-image-4232"/></a></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Since then, we've been joined by many teachers who offer prepared lessons and conversational ones, both in groups and one-on-one. During this journey, we've met wonderful students, some of whom we've even visited in person. Some of our students have become real friends and continued to support us. We've also published two books, the "Lebanese Arabic Phrasebook" volumes 1 and 2, and I've had the opportunity to teach live in some of Beirut's charming cafes.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Whether they came to impress their Lebanese partner, mother-in-law, boss, parents, or simply to try a new activity, our students have loved the courses and have been able to speak the language with ease. They've also been relieved to find that they can use Lebanese not only in Lebanon but throughout the Middle Eastern and North African (MENA) region.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Today, "Lebanese Arabic with Hiba" has expanded, but it will always maintain its casual, stress-free approach to teaching and learning. I want my students to be able to express themselves in Lebanese, understand jokes and food menus, while also learning grammar and conjugation. A bit of fun never hurts, whether during the course or in life!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>So, dear potential student, I'd be happy to teach you and share my approach to learning Lebanese. I look forward to sharing my love of Lebanese with you!</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Salam,<br>Hiba.</p>
